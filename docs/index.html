• The Fountain of Youth

    Continuation of the fan project by BYTE RANGER Software.

    >> This project is considered an non-profit fans video game project <<

    Indiana Jones and the Fountain of Youth is a freeware fan game project in a similar style to the 1992 classic Indiana Jones and the Fate of Atlantis. The game is in development and features brand new original art and animations in classic low-res style, a gripping story, advanced coding methods and original music.

    Features

    • Chapters include Bimini, Europe & Asia
    • Over 150 hand-drawn backgrounds (low-res look)
    • Classic "Atlantis" GUI & mechanics
    • Puzzles, action, and story
    • Antiquities, sidekicks & leather jackets!

  • History

    Origins (1999). Fountain of Youth began as a fan project conceived and scripted by Jocke Andersson around 1999. Over time the scope and ambition grew as more artists and developers joined the effort.

    First public demo (2006). After years of prototyping and asset work, a playable demo was released in 2006 and picked up by the games press and community.

    Side releases. The team produced seasonal mini-games and flash animations, including Temple of Spheres (2009) and Passage of Saints (2012).

    Chapter plan (2013). In 2013 the team decided to split the game into chapters for release, while continuing to recruit contributors to share the workload.

    Team. Over the years contributors included Mark Lovegrove (Screen 7), Misja van Laatum, Jan “DKH” Simon, Paul Wilkinson (Subspark), Mutbrecht Asengard and many community helpers.

    Project status (2021). By 2021 public posts indicated the project was discontinued under Screen 7.

    Continuation (2025). The project is now being continued by BYTE RANGER Software with the aim of releasing it in stages.

  • Development

    Engine/Framework: Adventure Game Studio (AGS)

    Resolution & Rendering

    • Native resolution: 640×400 (16:10). Classic framing with 2× the pixel density of 320×200.
    • Scaling: Aspect-preserving, nearest-neighbour; pillarbox/letterbox where needed.
    • Asset pipeline: Backgrounds (re-)drawn natively at 640×400; UI/fonts tuned for readability.

    Build & Tooling

    • AGS 3.6.x project, Windows builds via Editor; CI (GitHub Actions) for nightly zips.
    • Art: Aseprite/PS; Audio: Reaper/Audacity; Versioning: Git (LFS only for large original sources).

    Roadmap

    • M0 – Project Setup (vertical-slice tech): 1 test room (walkable areas & walk-behinds), verb-UI/inventory stub, one hotspot interaction, basic dialog, packaged build.
    • M1 – Core Gameplay: Rooms & transitions, hotspots/objects, inventory flow, dialog system, save/load, pathfinding, blocking volumes, options.
    • M2 – Bimini Prologue: 3–6 rooms, 5–8 puzzles, 1 mini-cutscene, first audio pass, QA; public demo.

    Notes

    • Deliver crisp pixels on modern displays; default to Nearest-Neighbour and aspect-preserving scale in setup.
    • Keep art guidelines (palette, line weight, anim FPS, font sizes) in ATTRIBUTION.md/docs/.
